# Scoring Rules v1

The scorecard is a structured rubric, not a precision model. Use conservative scoring. Disqualifiers override scores.

Total Score: **0–100**

## A. Market Structure (0–15)

High (12–15)
- Strong liquidity, healthy average volume, reasonable float.
- Institutional ownership supports legitimacy.
- Short interest is measurable, not pathological.

Medium (6–11)
- Tradable but not great liquidity.
- Float or ownership profile raises some caution.

Low (0–5)
- Thin volume, slippage risk.
- Microfloat chaos, inconsistent trading, hard to size positions safely.

## B. Financial Health (0–30)

High (22–30)
- Revenue is real and growing, margins stable or improving.
- FCF is positive or clearly trending toward positive.
- Cash position supports operations without near-term dilution.

Medium (12–21)
- Revenue exists but growth or margins are inconsistent.
- FCF negative but runway is reasonable and improving.

Low (0–11)
- Weak revenue quality, declining growth, collapsing margins.
- No credible path to profitability. Runway pressure.

## C. Balance Sheet & Survival Risk (0–20)

High (16–20)
- No going concern language.
- Strong cash relative to burn.
- Debt manageable. Dilution limited.

Medium (8–15)
- Some leverage or recent raises.
- Runway 12–24 months.
- Dilution risk exists but not constant.

Low (1–7)
- Runway <12 months.
- Heavy debt stress or frequent capital raises.
- Dilution likely.

Zero (0)
- Going concern flag present. Refer to disqualifiers.

## D. Market Behavior & Sentiment (0–15)

High (11–15)
- Stable trend or constructive basing with improving volume.
- Volatility is present but not chaotic.
- No recent dilution shocks.

Medium (6–10)
- Mixed trend, high volatility, sentiment uncertain.
- Some dilution or event-driven moves.

Low (0–5)
- Persistent downtrend.
- Volatility spikes tied to financing, reverse splits, or promotional cycles.

## E. Policy & Capital Behavior (0–10)

v1 intent: small weight, confirmatory only.

High (7–10)
- Clear, repeated aligned signals (insider buys, policy tailwinds, credible capital behavior).
- Must be explained, not assumed.

Medium (3–6)
- Some signal, weak clustering, or unclear alignment.

Low (0–2)
- No meaningful signal.

## F. Narrative & Optionality (0–10)

High (7–10)
- Clear catalyst path with credible execution.
- Acquisition optionality or structural tailwind supported by evidence.

Medium (3–6)
- Narrative exists but needs proof.
- Optionality is plausible but uncertain.

Low (0–2)
- Pure story, no evidence, or mismatched incentives.

## Risk Tier Mapping (Guidance)

80–100: Low / Medium risk. DD Priority
65–79: Medium risk. Track
50–64: High risk. Parking Lot
<50: Pass unless explicitly speculative
